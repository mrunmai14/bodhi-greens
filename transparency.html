Our Accountability Principles

Five commitments we make to you.

01
We publish what we can verify
We never claim impact we have not measured. Where data is self-assessed, we say so. Where it is aspirational, we call it a hypothesis. We believe honest reporting builds more trust than optimistic fiction.
02
We welcome independent scrutiny
Our accounts are externally audited. We invite programme evaluations from independent researchers. We do not hide from difficult findings — we publish them.
03
Donor funds are protected
100% of restricted grants are used for the designated programme. Overhead costs are funded separately and disclosed in full. We do not cross-subsidise without explicit consent.
04
We disclose conflicts of interest
All board members and staff declare conflicts of interest annually. Procurement decisions involving related parties are reviewed by the full executive committee.

Yes. Bodhi Greens Foundation is registered as CSR eligible under Schedule VII of the Companies Act, 2013. 12A registration is granted by the Income Tax Department of India to nonprofit organisations. It confers charitable institution status under Section 12A of the Income Tax Act 1961, meaning the organisation's income is exempt from tax and it can receive donations and grants under a formal charitable framework.
